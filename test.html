About

Mission: Our goal is to introduce coding to groups
who generally aren't exposed to coding as much or
who don't have easy access to hackathons.
Specifically, we are targeting minorities and
residents of the Tri-Valley area.

FAQ

How do you register?

You can sign up using this link: trivalleycryptohacks.com. We are asking for a ten dollar fee to cover food costs. Additionally, please fill out the MANDATORY liability form.

What do you bring?

You need to bring your laptop, as we are not providing laptops. We suggest you bring your chargers (for your phone and laptop), liability waiver, sleeping bag/pillow, any medication, and reusable water bottles.

Is there a fee and what for?

We are charging 10 dollars for shirt and 3 meals plus many snacks throughout the event.

Who can come?

This hackathon is open to all high schoolers all over Bay Area, especially from the Tri-Valley Area.

Will we have food, and if so what are the plans?

We are planning a lunch after arrival, an afternoon snack, dinner, a midnight snack and a breakfast. We will also have extra snacks throughout the event.

Will there be any other activities, workshops, or games?

There will be an intro to HTML/CSS workshop designed for beginner audiences. In the workshop, you will get a brief overview of web design and will design your first basic website.We are planning to have a tournament that is a surprise! We will let you know at the time of the game what we are playing for a competition. Yes, you will get a prize if you win.

Is there a layout of the schedule?

Yes! If you look on this website under the schedule, you can see a layout of the full 24 hours of the hackathon.

What is the location and time?

It's at Oracle in Pleasanton (5815 Owens Dr, Pleasanton, CA 94588) from April 20th to April 21st, 12pm to 12pm.

Why should you come?

Free food, free stuff, cool workshops, games, and prizes

ISACA is an independent, nonprofit, and global association. ISACA engages in the development, globally accepted, and industry-leading knowledge and practices for information systems. Isaca was previously known as the Information Systems Audit and Control Association, but was changed to the acronym to reflect the broad range of IT governance professionals it serves. ISACA formed the ITGI to focus on original research, publications, resources and symposia on IT governance and related topics. In addition to the work carried out by the ITGI. ISACA also addresses the topic through conference sessions and tracks and also education courses. ISACA offers different certificated including CISA (Certified Information Systems Audtior), CISM (Certified Information System Manager), CGEIT (Certified in the Governance of Enterprise IT), and the CRISC (Certified in Risk and Information Systems Control).
